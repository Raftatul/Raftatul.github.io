The Atrium - Unreal Engine 5 Blueprints

3C

The Atrium is a team school project made in 2 months. I have contribute to the design of the 3C and coding it.

  • Camera

    The camera was designed to feel alive and dynamic without giving the sensation of motion sickness.

    Here is a list of elements implement:

    • Camera tilt: The camera tilt a little based on the x velocity relative to the camera of the player.
    • Camera Bobbing: Apply camera bobbing when the player is moving on the ground using the Camera Shake system of Unreal Engine.
      The shake force depend of the run state of the player.
    • FOV: The FOV increase when the player fall to enhance the sense of speed.

  • Character - Controller

    I worked on the bump ability and the movements of the controller.

    Bump Ability:

    • The player can push himself in the opposite direction he is facing when bumping on certain surfaces.
    • There is a max amount of 3 charges, the charges reload when the player touch the ground.

UI

I implement the bump ability feedback:

  • The player is notice he can bump when the white dot appear.
  • An animation is played when the player use the bump input.
  • When a bump is successfully made, a charge is removed (turning gray).

Save System - Settings

I worked on the save system, it register the player position, time and settings.

I implement the graphics, audio and gameplay settings. Each parameters are saved by the Save System.

Steam Integration

I implement Steam using the Steam SDK and Unreal C++ to be able to use the Steam leaderboard system for the game. (Not working perfectly because it's not released on Steam yet.)

Fog

The level artists Rémi Trotereau and Charle Defoy asked me to create a fog that can alternate between multiple colors so they could adjust the fog's color according to the player's height.

Here is what I made to accomplish this effect:

  • First, I search the color range based on the z position of the camera.
  • Then, I blend the material color based on a lerp between the colors and the camera z position.
  • Finally, I applied the color.
